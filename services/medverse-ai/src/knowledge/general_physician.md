# General Physician — Clinical Reference Knowledge

## Vital Sign Reference Ranges (Adult)
| Parameter | Normal | Concerning | Critical |
|-----------|--------|-----------|----------|
| Heart Rate | 60–100 BPM | 40–60 or 100–130 | <40 or >130 |
| Respiratory Rate | 12–20/min | 8–12 or 20–25 | <8 or >25 |
| SpO₂ | 95–100% | 90–94% | <90% |
| Systolic BP | 90–140 mmHg | 80–90 or 140–180 | <80 or >180 |
| Temperature | 36.1–37.2°C | 37.3–38.5 or 35–36 | >38.5 or <35 |
| MAP | 70–105 mmHg | 60–70 | <60 (organ ischemia) |

## Sepsis Screening (qSOFA)
- Altered mental status (GCS <15)
- Respiratory rate ≥22/min
- Systolic BP ≤100 mmHg
- **≥2 criteria**: Suspect sepsis → lactate, blood cultures, antibiotics within 1 hour

## Cross-Specialty Red Flag Integration
- **Cardio + Pulmonary**: Tachycardia + desaturation → PE or cardiogenic shock
- **Neuro + Cardio**: Bradycardia + hypertension + irregular breathing → Cushing's triad
- **Derm + Neuro**: Diaphoresis + altered consciousness → hypoglycemia, autonomic crisis
- **OB + Cardio**: Maternal tachycardia + fetal decelerations → placental abruption
- **Ocular + Neuro**: Unequal pupils + GCS drop → transtentorial herniation

## Triage Protocol (ESI — Emergency Severity Index)
| Level | Description | Response Time |
|-------|------------|---------------|
| ESI-1 | Resuscitation | Immediate |
| ESI-2 | Emergent | <10 minutes |
| ESI-3 | Urgent | 30 minutes |
| ESI-4 | Less urgent | 60 minutes |
| ESI-5 | Non-urgent | 120 minutes |

## Pain Assessment (Numeric Rating Scale)
- **0**: No pain
- **1–3**: Mild (reassurance, non-pharmacological)
- **4–6**: Moderate (analgesics, monitoring)
- **7–10**: Severe (urgent pain management, evaluate cause)

## Polypharmacy Considerations
- Flag if >5 concurrent medications on patient record
- Check drug-drug interactions for all critical combinations
- Monitor renal/hepatic function for dose adjustments
- Age-adjusted dosing for pediatric and geriatric populations

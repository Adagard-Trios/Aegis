# Neurology & Biomechanics — Clinical Reference Knowledge

## Glasgow Coma Scale (GCS)
| Component | Response | Score |
|-----------|----------|-------|
| Eye Opening | Spontaneous / To voice / To pain / None | 4/3/2/1 |
| Verbal | Oriented / Confused / Inappropriate / Incomprehensible / None | 5/4/3/2/1 |
| Motor | Obeys / Localizes / Withdraws / Flexion / Extension / None | 6/5/4/3/2/1 |
- **Total**: 3–15. Severe ≤8, Moderate 9–12, Mild 13–15
- **Intubated patients**: Use GCS-M (motor component only)

## Posture & Spinal Assessment
- **Sagittal balance**: C7 plumb line within ±2cm of S1 posterior edge = balanced
- **Cervical lordosis**: Normal 20–40° (C2–C7 Cobb angle)
- **Thoracic kyphosis**: Normal 20–45°
- **Lumbar lordosis**: Normal 40–60°
- **Scoliosis screening**: Cobb angle >10° = scoliosis. >25° = monitoring, >40° = surgical consideration

## Fall Risk Assessment (Morse Fall Scale)
| Factor | Score |
|--------|-------|
| History of falling | 25 |
| Secondary diagnosis | 15 |
| Ambulatory aid | 15–30 |
| IV/Heparin lock | 20 |
| Gait: impaired | 10–20 |
| Mental status: forgets limitations | 15 |
- **Risk Level**: 0–24 low, 25–44 moderate, ≥45 high risk

## IMU-Based Motion Analysis
- **Accelerometer norms**: Static <0.05g deviation from gravity vector = stable posture
- **Gyroscope**: Angular velocity >300°/s in free fall signature → fall event
- **Impact detection**: Resultant acceleration >3g followed by posture change → confirmed fall
- **Tremor detection**: Resting tremor 4–6 Hz (Parkinson's), Essential tremor 8–12 Hz, Physiological <4 Hz

## Sleep Stage Criteria
- **Wake**: Alpha waves (8–13 Hz) with eye blinks, high muscle tone
- **N1 (Light)**: Theta waves (4–7 Hz), slow eye movements
- **N2**: Sleep spindles (12–14 Hz bursts), K-complexes
- **N3 (Deep/SWS)**: Delta waves (0.5–4 Hz, >75μV), minimal movement
- **REM**: Low voltage mixed frequency, rapid eye movements, muscle atonia

## Neurological Red Flags
- GCS drop of ≥2 points → urgent CT/MRI
- Sudden unilateral weakness, speech difficulty → stroke protocol (FAST)
- Acute fall with loss of consciousness > 5 minutes
- New-onset seizure in monitored patient
- Fixed dilated pupil (unilateral) → herniation syndrome

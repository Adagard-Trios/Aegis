# Cardiology — Clinical Reference Knowledge

## ECG Interpretation
- **Normal Sinus Rhythm (NSR)**: Rate 60–100 BPM, regular P waves before every QRS, PR interval 0.12–0.20s, QRS < 0.12s
- **Sinus Bradycardia**: Rate < 60 BPM with normal morphology. Evaluate for medications (beta-blockers), athlete's heart, hypothyroidism
- **Sinus Tachycardia**: Rate > 100 BPM. Consider pain, fever, dehydration, anxiety, hyperthyroidism, anemia
- **Atrial Fibrillation (AFib)**: Irregularly irregular rhythm, absent P waves, fibrillatory baseline. CHA₂DS₂-VASc for stroke risk
- **Atrial Flutter**: Sawtooth F waves (rate ~300/min), often 2:1 block → ventricular rate ~150 BPM
- **Ventricular Tachycardia (VT)**: Wide QRS (>0.12s), rate >100 BPM. If sustained >30s or hemodynamically unstable → emergency
- **Ventricular Fibrillation (VF)**: Chaotic undulations, no organized QRS → cardiac arrest, immediate defibrillation

## ST-Segment Analysis
- **ST Elevation**: ≥1mm in limb leads, ≥2mm in precordial leads → suspect STEMI. Evaluate contiguous lead groups
- **ST Depression**: ≥0.5mm horizontal/downsloping → ischemia, NSTEMI. Reciprocal changes support acute MI
- **Wellens Syndrome**: Deep symmetric T-wave inversions or biphasic T-waves in V2–V3 → critical LAD stenosis

## Heart Rate Variability (HRV)
- **SDNN (Standard Deviation of NN intervals)**: Normal >100ms. <50ms indicates severely reduced autonomic function
- **RMSSD**: Reflects parasympathetic (vagal) tone. Normal >20ms
- **LF/HF Ratio**: Low-frequency to high-frequency power ratio. >2.0 suggests sympathetic dominance

## Cardiac Biomarker Thresholds
- **Troponin I/T**: >99th percentile URL = myocardial injury. Serial rise/fall pattern = acute MI
- **BNP**: >100 pg/mL suggests heart failure. >400 pg/mL = high probability of CHF
- **NT-proBNP**: Age-stratified: <300 pg/mL rules out HF; >450 (age<50), >900 (50-75), >1800 (>75) = HF likely

## Blood Pressure Classification (AHA/ACC 2017)
| Category | Systolic | Diastolic |
|----------|----------|-----------|
| Normal | <120 | <80 |
| Elevated | 120–129 | <80 |
| Stage 1 HTN | 130–139 | 80–89 |
| Stage 2 HTN | ≥140 | ≥90 |
| Hypertensive Crisis | >180 | >120 |

## Red Flags Requiring Immediate Escalation
- Sustained VT or VF on telemetry
- Acute ST elevation in ≥2 contiguous leads
- Heart rate <40 or >150 with hemodynamic compromise
- New-onset complete heart block (3rd degree AV block)
- Troponin rising pattern with chest pain

# Pulmonology — Clinical Reference Knowledge

## Respiratory Rate Classification
- **Normal Adult**: 12–20 breaths/min
- **Tachypnea**: >20 breaths/min. Consider: anxiety, fever, metabolic acidosis, PE, pneumonia
- **Bradypnea**: <12 breaths/min. Consider: opioid toxicity, CNS depression, hypothyroidism
- **Apnea**: Cessation >10 seconds. Pathological if recurrent → evaluate for central/obstructive sleep apnea

## Oxygen Saturation (SpO₂) Interpretation
| SpO₂ Range | Classification | Action |
|------------|---------------|--------|
| 95–100% | Normal | Monitoring |
| 90–94% | Mild Hypoxemia | Supplemental O₂, investigate cause |
| 85–89% | Moderate Hypoxemia | Urgent O₂ therapy, ABG |
| <85% | Severe Hypoxemia | Emergency intervention, intubation considered |

- **Note**: SpO₂ may be unreliable in: poor perfusion, nail polish, carbon monoxide poisoning, severe anemia, dark pigmentation

## ARDS Berlin Criteria (2012)
- **Timing**: Within 1 week of known insult or new/worsening respiratory symptoms
- **Imaging**: Bilateral opacities not fully explained by effusions/atelectasis
- **Origin**: Not fully explained by cardiac failure or fluid overload
- **Severity by PaO₂/FiO₂**:
  - Mild: 200–300 mmHg (PEEP ≥5)
  - Moderate: 100–200 mmHg (PEEP ≥5)
  - Severe: ≤100 mmHg (PEEP ≥5)

## Breath Sound Patterns
- **Crackles (Rales)**: Fine = pulmonary fibrosis, early pneumonia. Coarse = bronchitis, CHF fluid
- **Wheezing**: High-pitched = bronchospasm (asthma, COPD). Fixed wheeze = consider obstruction
- **Rhonchi**: Low-pitched rumbling = secretions in large airways
- **Stridor**: Inspiratory = upper airway obstruction. Emergency if progressive
- **Absent/Diminished**: Pneumothorax, pleural effusion, severe COPD with air trapping

## Pulmonary Function Interpretation
- **FEV1/FVC ratio**: <0.70 = obstructive pattern (COPD, asthma)
- **FVC reduced, FEV1/FVC normal/high**: Restrictive pattern (fibrosis, chest wall disease)
- **Bronchodilator response**: >12% and >200mL improvement in FEV1 = reversible obstruction (asthma)

## Capnography (EtCO₂)
- **Normal**: 35–45 mmHg
- **Hypocapnia (<35)**: Hyperventilation, anxiety, PE, metabolic acidosis compensation
- **Hypercapnia (>45)**: Hypoventilation, COPD exacerbation, sedation, respiratory failure

## Red Flags Requiring Immediate Escalation
- SpO₂ <85% or rapidly declining
- Respiratory rate >30 or <8
- Stridor with accessory muscle use
- Sudden desaturation with pleuritic chest pain → suspect PE
- Silent chest in known asthmatic → near-fatal asthma
